My favourite russian recipes

Kholodets

Ingredients:

  • 2 lb pork or beef
  • 1 onion
  • 2 carrots
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 10 peppercorns
  • 1/4 cup vinegar
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Place the meat, onion, carrots, bay leaves, peppercorns, and vinegar in a large pot.
  2. Add enough water to cover the ingredients and bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce heat to low and simmer for 2-3 hours, or until the meat is tender.
  4. Remove the meat from the pot and let it cool.
  5. Once cooled, remove any bones and cut the meat into small cubes.
  6. Return the meat to the pot and add salt to taste.
  7. Let the mixture cool in the fridge for at least 4 hours or overnight.
  8. Serve chilled and enjoy!

Seledka pod Shuboy

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb herring fillets
  • 1 onion
  • 1 apple
  • 1 carrot
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 1/4 cup sour cream
  • 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Soak the herring fillets in cold water for at least 2 hours to remove excess salt.
  2. Drain the fillets and pat dry.
  3. Finely chop the onion, apple, and carrot.
  4. In a bowl, mix the mayonnaise, sour cream, mustard, sugar, salt, and pepper.
  5. Add the chopped vegetables and mix well.
  6. Add the herring fillets to the mixture and toss to coat evenly.
  7. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours.
  8. Serve chilled and enjoy!

Pelmeni

Ingredients:

  • 1 large egg
  • 1 teaspoon vegetable
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ¾ cup warm water, or more as needed
  • 3 cups all purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon all purpose flour for dusting
  • 18 ounces ground beef
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 1½ tablespoons ice-cold water
  • 1 tablespoon coarse salt
  • freshly ground pepper to taste
  1. Make dough: Combine egg, oil, and salt in a liquid measuring cup; add enough warm water to fill to 1 cup.
    Pour into a large bowl and knead in 3 cups flour until smooth and elastic.
    Cover with a dish towel and let dough rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Meanwhile, make filling: Combine ground beef, onion, water, salt, and pepper in a medium bowl;
    mix thoroughly by hand or using a fork. Set aside.
  3. Dust a baking sheet lightly with 1 tablespoon flour; set aside.
  4. Roll out a portion of dough very thinly on a lightly floured surface;
    keep remaining dough covered with a dish towel to avoid drying out.
    Cut out 2 1/2-inch rounds with a cookie cutter.
    Place about 1 teaspoon filling on one side of each dough circle.
    Fold dough over and seal the edges using your fingers, forming a crescent.
    Join the ends and pinch them together. Place on the prepared baking sheet.
    Repeat with remaining dough and filling.
    Freeze pelmeni for 30 minutes to prevent them from sticking together.
  5. Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a simmer.
    Working in batches, cook frozen pelmeni in simmering water until meat is cooked through and pelmeni float to the top,
    about 5 minutes. Continue cooking for 5 more minutes.
    Transfer to serving plates using a slotted spoon.
